*State as of the M2.2/M2.3 combat slice + dwell-time escalation + pause. This is

---

## 1. Orientation

The indoor world is the Castle-of-Bane interior, de-windowed and hosted as a
shell guest. A **building is a stack of floors**; a **floor is a `DungeonMap`**;
moving between floors is internal to `IndoorWorld` (a staircase swap), never a
portal transition. The portal crosses exactly one seam — outdoor↔indoor —
carrying only `PlayerState`. Coordinates, the vertical axis, and the enemy roster
never cross it.

Coordinate space is Bane-native and **sealed** inside `indoor/` and its renderer:
**−Y is up** (floor `y=0`, eye `y=−15`, ceiling `y≈−60`), human scale
(`CELL_SIZE=50`), heading in degrees about +Y, forward `= (sin h, −cos h)`. The
renderer applies a `glScalef(1,−1,1)` so −Y reads as up on screen. If you add
anything that draws or moves in here, it lives in this space; nothing about it
leaks across the portal.

## 3. What's shipped

- **M2.0 base** — real grid dungeon, collision, slide-along, grid LOS, prompt-gated
  stairs (U/I), floor-0 exit → M3 outcome payload (`from`/`cleared`/`depth`/
  `found`/`hint`). Objectives (plant/intel) placed and walk-over collected.
- **M2.2 melee** — three baked clone-mobsters. Thug + knifeman chase (LOS-gated
  aggro → face → slide-along approach) and slash on cadence; damage routes into
  the shared `PlayerState` pool (respawn grace + life-spend mirror the outdoor
  economy). Player **strike** on `fire` (Space): forward-arc, reach, cadence-gated,
  culls on kill. Inter-mob **separation** so a crowd fans into an arc, never fuses.
- **M2.3 ranged** — the gunman: standoff/kite positioning, LOS- and cadence-gated
  fire of a slow `Bolt` the world flies (lands on the player, spends on a wall,
  expires, or voids on a floor swap). `LIVE_ROSTER` is melee-weighted (~20% gunman).
- **Dwell-time escalation** — a populated building sends reinforcements onto the
  current floor, the interval ramping from ~18s down to ~6s over ~80s of dwell, up
  to a per-floor cap. The *intra-dive* ramp; composes with `tier` (the cross-dive
  baseline). Per-dive (resets on `on_enter`), persists across floors, inert on the
  bare default stack.
- **Pause + frame-step** — shell-level freeze of both worlds; `.` advances exactly
  one 16 ms tick for studying fast motion (built to "catch" the bolt).

## 4. Contracts (do not break)

1. **Sealed −Y-up space.** Floor `y=0`, up is negative y. Anything drawn or moved
   in `indoor/` obeys this; the renderer's `glScalef(1,−1,1)` is the only flip.
2. **One damage pool.** All damage — melee slash, gunman bolt — goes through
   `PlayerState.take_damage`; a pool-emptying hit calls `lose_life`. Never mutate
   health/lives anywhere else. Game-over freezes `_update_enemies`.
3. **Determinism.** Placement (objectives, enemies) and reinforcement waves are
   seeded by the building seed with pure-int arithmetic — a re-dive of the same
   building reproduces loot, layout, and waves. Keep new randomness seeded the
   same way (distinct salt, no `hash()` of str/tuple).
4. **Bare stack stays inert.** The `{"building": id}` payload (no `archetype`)
   takes the default test stack: no objectives, no enemies, no reinforcement. The
   M2.0 / floor-stack pins assert its exact geometry — do not populate it.
5. **Numpy-free runtime.** `models/mobsters.py` is baked tuples. numpy lives only
   in `tools/`. To change a body: tune the tool, eyeball in `monster_viewer`,
   re-run `python -m az.tools.bake_mobsters`.
6. **Transient lifetimes.** Bolts and (future) FX are active-floor-only and void
   on a floor swap (`_apply_floor` clears `_bolts`). Mobs on an off-floor persist
   (frozen) and resume on return; reinforcement heat persists building-wide.

## 6. TODO — next sessions

Ordered by value-per-effort. Each: **what / why / where / fork.**

### Tier 1 — close the combat *feel* (small, highest payoff)

- **T1. First-person staff overlay + strike feedback.** *The strike is mechanically
  live but invisible — no weapon in view, no swing, no hit confirmation, so melee
  reads as "enemies just vanish."* This is the single biggest feel gap. **Where:**
  a screen-space overlay pass in `renderer.py` after the world draw and before the
  HUD; `world.py` exposes a swing-timer/strike-state the overlay reads (the
  `_strike_cd` is already there to drive it). Castle of Bane shipped a first-person
  staff overlay — vendor its idiom. **Fork:** full animated staff swing vs. a cheap
  arc-flash + hit spark. Start with the cheap version; it sells the hit.

- **T2. Indoor kill scoring.** *Killing a mob awards nothing — score sits at 000000
  indoors while a tank kill scores outdoors. The reward loop is missing.* **Where:**
  the cull/`hit`-kill path in `world.py._player_strike` (and any future damage
  source); `state.add_score(points)` with a per-`EnemyDef` point value (gunman/
  knifeman worth more than thug). Consider carrying indoor kills in the M3 outcome
  payload so the outdoor side can read a "fought through" signal. Small.

- **T3. Bolt impact + muzzle feedback.** *Gunman fire has no muzzle flash; a bolt
  hit shows only the existing damage-flash border. Ranged reads thin.* **Where:** a
  shared transient-FX list on the world (parallel to `_bolts`) spawned on fire (a
  muzzle spark at the gunman) and on hit (an impact burst at the player); a
  `_draw_fx` in `renderer.py`. Reuse the bolt lifetime pattern. Pairs with T1 (one
  FX system serves staff hits and bolt impacts).

### Tier 2 — mechanics depth

- **T4. Mob aggro memory / search.** *Aggro is pure LOS-gated: a mob idles the
  instant LOS breaks, so rounding a corner makes it forget you mid-stride — reads
  as dumb.* **Where:** `mob.py` — add a `last_seen` point + a short memory timer; on
  LOS loss, pursue last-seen for N ticks, then idle. **Fork:** keep pure-LOS (it's
  the current dodge mechanic — breaking sight *is* the escape) vs. add memory.
  Recommend a *short* memory: still lets LOS be the escape, but the mob commits to
  the corner for a beat instead of snapping off. Don't over-pursue or the gunman's
  LOS-counter (duck behind cover) stops working.

- **T5. Player↔mob body collision.** *The player walks through mobs — only walls
  block the player; mobs separate from each other but don't block you. A thug you
  can phase through undercuts the melee threat.* **Where:** fold mob circles into
  the player's `_blocked`/`can_move_to` in `world.py._sim_tick`. **Fork:** block
  (tactile, but risks trapping the player in a crowd — the separation pass mitigates
  this) vs. soft-push. Recommend block with the existing slide-along.

### Tier 3 — systems (larger, cross-cutting)

- **T6. Tier ratchet wiring (cross-ref: outdoor).** *Intra-dive escalation (heat)
  is done; the cross-dive `tier` that sets a building's starting difficulty is read
  by placement but the **outdoor return-from-dive ratchet that bumps `tier` is still
  a placeholder** (`tier = len(cleared)`-ish). Indoor difficulty scaling only comes
  alive once that's real.* This is an outdoor task, listed here because it gates the
  indoor difficulty curve. **Where:** `outdoor/world.py.on_enter` (the ratchet);
  no indoor change needed once `tier` moves for real.

- **T7. Tier-driven roster + reinforcement.** *`LIVE_ROSTER` is fixed and
  reinforcement pacing/cap is tier-independent — high-tier buildings aren't meaner
  in kind, only in starting count.* **Where:** `enemy_placement.py` (roster mix by
  tier — more gunmen / tougher weighting deep), `world.py` (`_reinforce_interval` /
  `REINFORCE_CAP` scaled by tier). Depends on T6 to be observable.

- **T8. Audio (future system).** *No sound anywhere — fire, hit, footstep, death,
  the reinforcement arrival.* A whole subsystem, not a session. Flag only: the
  reinforcement spawn is deliberately silent and out-of-sight, which without an
  audio cue can feel like a teleport ambush (see T4/feel). An arrival sound is the
  cheap fix when audio lands.

### Tier 4 — polish / debt

- **T9. Rename `placement.py` vs `enemy_placement.py`.** *The near-identical names
  (both with a private `_reserved`) already caused a clobber-and-crash. A footgun.*
  Rename the objectives one (e.g. `objectives.py`) and update the two import sites

- **T10. Stale window title.** `shell/app.py` still sets "Auto Warfare — M0 walking

- **T11. Mob render perf (watch, don't pre-optimize).** Baked bodies are ~1.3k

- **T12. Bolt directional tracer (deferred look).** The 3-axis cross reads fine at

  streak would foreshorten to a dot head-on. Deferred — only if the cross bugs you
  up close. `renderer._draw_bolt` + pass bolt velocity from `world.draw`.
